# Editor UI Workflows

## Setting up the game scene environment

### Asset Management

<table><thead><tr><th width="284">Asset Action</th><th>Steps to perform Action</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Adding an Asset</td><td><ol><li>Select Quick Access Menu and click on the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Asset</code></mark> tab. An expanded Asset Library opens up</li><li>Search for the right asset by scrolling through the library or entering a keyword for the asset</li><li>Click on the desired asset and drag &#x26; drop it on to the scene</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Selecting an Asset</td><td><ol><li>Locate the asset visually in the scene and click on it. A yellow highlight shader shows up on the outline of the shader</li><li>Alternatively, Go to Layers > Select the asset from the layer if you know the asset's name</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Deleting an Asset</td><td><ol><li>Select asset  >  Press <code>Ctrl + Del</code>  or <code>Cmd + Del</code></li><li>Alternatively, Select asset > Goto the Asset in the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark>  > Right Click on Asset > Select <code>Del</code></li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Duplicating an Asset</td><td><ol><li>Select asset > Goto the asset in the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark>  > Right Click on Asset > Select <code>Duplicate</code></li><li>Alternatively, Select asset > Goto the Asset in the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark>  > Click on the Duplicate  Icon next to it</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Renaming an Asset in Layers</td><td>Select asset > Goto the asset in the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark>  > Click on the Edit Icon next to it > Rename the asset</td></tr><tr><td>Making an Asset a Child of Another Asset</td><td>Select Asset > Goto the asset in the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark> > Right Click and Drag to the desired Parent Asset</td></tr><tr><td>Adding an Empty parent to an Asset</td><td>Select Asset > Goto the asset in the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark>  > Right Click on Asset > Select <code>Add Empty Parent</code></td></tr><tr><td>Moving an Asset in the Layers</td><td>Select Asset > Goto the asset in the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark> > Right Click and Drag to desired location</td></tr><tr><td>Moving an Asset</td><td>Click on Asset in the Scene > Click on the <mark style="color:red;"><code>Move</code></mark> Icon in the Asset Gizmo > Select Axes > Use mouse to move</td></tr><tr><td>Scaling and Asset</td><td>Click on Asset in the Scene > Click on the <mark style="color:red;"><code>Scale</code></mark> Icon in the Asset Gizmo > Select Axes > Use mouse to scale along that axis</td></tr><tr><td>Rotating an Asset</td><td>Click on Asset in the Scene > Click on the <mark style="color:red;"><code>Rotate</code></mark> Icon in the Asset Gizmo > Select Axes of Rotation > Use mouse to rotate along that axis</td></tr></tbody></table>

### SFX & Particle Management

<table><thead><tr><th width="286">SFX &#x26; Particle Management Action</th><th>Steps to Perform Action</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Adding an SFX</td><td><ol><li>Select Quick Access Menu and click on the <mark style="color:green;"><code>SFX</code></mark> tab. An expanded SFX Library opens up</li><li>Preview the SFX by clicking on the Play icon </li><li>Add the SFX by clicking on the + button</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Customizing SFX</td><td><ol><li>Select the added SFX from the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark></li><li>Set the Advanced Mode toggle to 'on' state. You will view the Inspector Panel for SFX</li><li>Customize the properties for the SFX</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Adding a VFX</td><td><p></p><ol><li>Select Quick Access Menu and click on the <mark style="color:green;"><code>SFX</code></mark> tab. An expanded Particles Library opens up</li><li>Scroll through the particles preview</li><li>Add the particle VFX by clicking on the + button</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Customizing VFX</td><td><p></p><ol><li>Select the added Particle VFX from the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Layers</code></mark></li><li>Set the Advanced Mode toggle to 'on' state. You will view the Inspector Panel for Particle VFX</li><li>Customize the properties for the Particle VFX</li></ol></td></tr></tbody></table>

### Scene Management

<table><thead><tr><th width="287">Scene Management Action</th><th>Steps to perform action</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Adding a New Scene</td><td><ol><li>Select Quick Access Menu and click on the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Scenes</code></mark> tab. </li><li>Click on the + icon to add a new scene</li><li>Set a name to the scene and press Enter</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Load a New Scene</td><td><ol><li>Select Quick Access Menu and click on the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Scenes</code></mark> tab. </li><li>Double Click on the scene you want to load on the editor</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Deleting an Existing Scene</td><td><p></p><ol><li>Select Quick Access Menu and click on the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Scenes</code></mark> tab. </li><li>Press the Delete icon next to the scene you want to delete</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Setting a Scene as Default Scene to load on game start</td><td><ol><li>Load the the Scene you want to set as default</li><li>Select Quick Access Menu and click on the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Scenes</code></mark> tab. </li><li>Right click on your current scene and click <code>Set Default</code></li></ol></td></tr></tbody></table>

## Adding Logic to the game

<table><thead><tr><th width="284">Logic Action</th><th>Steps to perform action</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Adding a Logic Template to an Asset GameObject</td><td><ol><li>Click on the asset</li><li>Select the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Logic</code></mark> Tab in the Quick Access Menu</li><li>Select the correct Logic Template </li><li>Drag and drop the template to the asset GameObject</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Creating a Script</td><td><ol><li>Select the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Scripts</code></mark> Tab in the Quick Access Menu</li><li>Click on the + button </li><li>Name your script. </li><li>Press <code>Enter</code></li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Adding a Script to an Asset GameObject</td><td><p></p><ol><li>Click the asset GameObject to which you want to add the script</li><li>Select the <mark style="color:green;"><code>Scripts</code></mark> Tab in the Quick Access Menu</li><li>Drag and drop the script to the asset</li><li>You will see the script added as a tab in the Inspector Panel on the right. You can add multiple scripts to your object</li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Creating Variables for GameObjects</td><td><ol><li>Add the script to the asset</li><li>An <mark style="color:yellow;"><code>Object Variables</code></mark> tab will show up in the Inspector Panel</li><li>You will be shown four fields - one each for String, Float, Int and GameObject. </li><li>For String, Float and Int , enter the name and the value </li><li>For GameObject , enter a name and drag the object whose reference is to be entered as the value. </li></ol></td></tr><tr><td>Opening, Viewing and Editing a Script</td><td><ol><li>Click on the Visual Studio Code icon in the Debug Panel of the Main Toolbar on top. VS Code Editor will open the entire project with a folder containing all the scripts</li><li>Select the script you want to open, view or edit from<br></li></ol></td></tr></tbody></table>
